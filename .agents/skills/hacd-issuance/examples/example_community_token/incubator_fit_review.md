# Incubator Fit Review: StackFire

## Verdict

Strong fit

## Why it fits HACD

- Supply is hard-capped by the number of available HACD lots, not by a contract parameter that anyone can change.
- The community membership framing maps naturally to HACD's per-unit identity model: one HACD = one membership slot.
- Formation cost in HAC creates a real cost basis reference visible on-chain, which distinguishes StackFire from a costless airdrop or free mint.
- Removing a stack lot burns the corresponding SFR units, which ties asset supply permanently to HACD containers.

## What HACD adds

- **PoW container**: Each of the 100 HACD lots is a PoW-mined container. Membership slots are not artificially created — they are tied to real mined assets.
- **Formation cost**: 50 HAC per HACD lot. Total formation cost reference is 5,000 HAC. This makes early participation visible and non-trivial.
- **Asset history**: Every formation event is inscribed on-chain. The community can see exactly which HACD lots participated and when.
- **Community formation**: The 1–10 HACD per participant cap ensures no single participant can absorb all lots. This distributes formation across the community.

## Main concerns

- Future utility (governance voting, channel gating) depends on development not yet complete. Copy must be careful not to promise these features as certain.
- The issuer is a single anonymous operator. HACD Labs should confirm whether a more accountable team structure is needed before approval.
- No designated address phase. While this suits a community project, HACD Labs should verify that the public launch can absorb 100 lots without centralization risk.

## Required issuer confirmations

- Confirm that 50 HAC per HACD is the final stack cost and not a placeholder.
- Confirm the maximum of 10 HACD per participant is enforced at the Launchpad level.
- Confirm that governance and channel gating are marked as planned rather than promised.
- Confirm the team / operator identity to HACD Labs privately before approval.

## Recommended next step

Proceed to stack design and launch spec generation. Mark governance utility as roadmap dependency in all public copy. HACD Labs internal review required before Launchpad publication.
