# StackFire (SFR) — Issuer FAQ

## Do I need HACD to participate?

Yes. Each Stack participation requires 1 HACD per lot according to the StackFire launch rules. Without HACD you cannot form SFR.

## Do I need HAC?

Yes. HAC is required for two things: the stack cost (50 HAC per HACD lot) and the Hacash network transaction fee. Make sure you have enough HAC for both before you begin.

## How much HAC do I need for 1 lot?

50 HAC for the stack cost plus a small network fee. The network fee varies and should be checked at the time of your transaction.

## How many lots can I take?

Between 1 and 10 HACD lots per participant.

## How many SFR will I receive?

10,000 SFR per HACD lot. If you Stack 3 HACD lots you will receive 30,000 SFR.

## Is there a reserved allocation for the team?

No. There are no reserved lots, no team allocation, and no designated address requirement. All 100 lots are available through the public phase on a first-come basis.

## What happens when all 100 lots are filled?

No more SFR can ever be formed. Total supply is permanently capped at 1,000,000 SFR. The Launchpad will show the issuance as completed.

## What happens if I remove my Stack?

Removing the Stack releases your HACD unit. However, the 10,000 SFR tied to that lot are permanently burned. Your HAC formation cost is not refunded. This is by design — it links SFR supply directly to active HACD containers.

## Is SFR listed on any exchange?

No listing is guaranteed. StackFire has not arranged any exchange listing. Do not assume SFR will be tradable on any platform after launch.

## Is this an investment product?

No. SFR is a HACD Stack Asset for community membership. No profit, yield, price floor, or investment return is guaranteed or implied.

## When will governance and channel access be available?

These are planned features that depend on future development. They are not available at launch and are not guaranteed. Follow official StackFire channels for updates.

## Where can I verify my formation?

All Stack formations are recorded on the Hacash blockchain. You can verify your lot on the Hacash explorer or the HACD Launchpad after your transaction is confirmed.
